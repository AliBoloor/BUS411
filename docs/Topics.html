Lecture Topics

This document lists the planned lecture topics and subtopics for the course.

Lecture 1: History and Role of Fixed Income Markets

1.1 What is fixed income?

  • Definition of fixed income securities
  • Debt versus equity financing
  • Main issuers and investors

1.2 Historical development of fixed income markets

  • Early sovereign borrowing
  • Development of government debt markets
  • Growth of corporate bond markets

1.3 Economic role of fixed income securities

  • Funding governments
  • Funding corporations
  • Saving, retirement, and liability management

Lecture 2: Time Value of Money and Discounting

2.1 Time value of money

  • Present value
  • Future value
  • Discount factors

2.2 Compounding conventions

  • Annual compounding
  • Semiannual compounding
  • Continuous compounding

2.3 Discounting fixed income cash flows

  • Cash flow timelines
  • Pricing as present value of future cash flows
  • Relation between discount rates and value

Lecture 3: Annuities and Liability Valuation

3.1 Fixed annuities

  • Ordinary annuities
  • Annuities due
  • Perpetuities

3.2 Variable annuities

  • Basic structure
  • Investment-linked cash flows
  • Valuation considerations

3.3 Defined benefit pension plans

  • Nature of pension liabilities
  • Discounting promised payments
  • Funding and valuation issues

3.4 Liability valuation

  • Present value of liabilities
  • Matching assets to liabilities
  • Liability sensitivity to interest rates

Lecture 4: Government Bonds

4.1 Types of government securities

  • Treasury bills
  • Treasury notes
  • Treasury bonds

4.2 Features of government bonds

  • Face value
  • Coupon rate
  • Maturity
  • Clean price and dirty price

4.3 Valuation of government bonds

  • Pricing from discounted cash flows
  • Yield to maturity
  • Accrued interest

4.4 Government bond markets

  • Primary market
  • Secondary market
  • Benchmark role of sovereign debt

Lecture 5: Corporate Bonds and Credit Risk

5.1 Corporate bond features

  • Secured and unsecured bonds
  • Seniority
  • Covenants

5.2 Valuation of corporate bonds

  • Pricing with promised cash flows
  • Credit spread over government rates
  • Yield spread interpretation

5.3 Credit risk

  • Default risk
  • Recovery risk
  • Credit ratings

5.4 Corporate bond market considerations

  • Liquidity
  • Issuer quality
  • Term to maturity and risk

Lecture 6: Yield Measures and the Term Structure of Interest Rates

6.1 Yield measures

  • Current yield
  • Yield to maturity
  • Holding period return

6.2 Spot and forward rates

  • Spot rates
  • Forward rates
  • Discount function

6.3 Yield curve construction and interpretation

  • Normal, flat, and inverted curves
  • Economic interpretation
  • Expectations about future rates

6.4 Term structure concepts

  • Expectations view
  • Liquidity preference
  • Market segmentation intuition

Lecture 7: Duration

7.1 Interest rate risk in fixed income

  • Bond price and yield relationship
  • Why prices fall when yields rise

7.2 Macaulay duration

  • Definition
  • Weighted average time to cash flow receipt
  • Interpretation

7.3 Modified duration

  • Definition
  • Approximate price sensitivity
  • Duration as first-order risk measure

7.4 Determinants of duration

  • Coupon
  • Maturity
  • Yield level

Lecture 8: Convexity

8.1 Limits of duration-only approximation

  • Nonlinearity in bond prices
  • Curvature of the price-yield relationship

8.2 Convexity measure

  • Definition
  • Interpretation
  • Why convexity matters

8.3 Duration-convexity approximation

  • First-order and second-order effects
  • Improved bond price change estimates
  • Application to larger yield changes

8.4 Comparing bonds using convexity

  • Price sensitivity differences
  • Trade-off between yield and convexity
  • Portfolio implications

Lecture 9: Fixed Income Risk Management

9.1 Duration-based risk management

  • Measuring portfolio interest rate exposure
  • Portfolio duration

9.2 Immunization

  • Asset-liability matching
  • Duration matching
  • Protecting against small parallel shifts in yields

9.3 Convexity in risk management

  • Why duration matching alone is not enough
  • Role of convexity in immunization
  • Rebalancing issues

9.4 Practical limitations

  • Non-parallel yield curve shifts
  • Model risk
  • Transaction costs

Lecture 10: Bonds with Contingencies

10.1 Bonds with embedded options

  • Callable bonds
  • Putable bonds
  • Convertible features as a general idea

10.2 Valuation issues for contingent bonds

  • Cash flows depend on future events
  • Option-like features in bond valuation
  • Impact on price and yield

10.3 Mortgage-backed and prepayable securities

  • Prepayment risk
  • Negative convexity
  • Valuation challenges

10.4 Risk characteristics

  • Reinvestment risk
  • Call risk
  • Extension and contraction risk

Lecture 11: Fixed Income Derivative Securities

11.1 Overview of fixed income derivatives

  • Why derivatives are used
  • Hedging, speculation, and portfolio management

11.2 Interest rate forwards and futures

  • Basic contract structure
  • Hedging interest rate exposure
  • Relation to underlying fixed income securities

11.3 Interest rate swaps

  • Fixed-for-floating exchange
  • Basic valuation intuition
  • Uses in risk management

11.4 Options on interest rates

  • Caps
  • Floors
  • Basic role in managing rate risk

Coverage of Core Course Themes

The course is organized around three major themes:

1. History of fixed income securities

  • Covered primarily in Lecture 1

2. Valuation methods for fixed income securities

  • Fixed and variable annuities: Lecture 3
  • Government bonds: Lecture 4
  • Corporate bonds: Lecture 5
  • Defined benefit pension plans: Lecture 3
  • Bonds with contingencies: Lecture 10
  • Fixed income derivative securities: Lecture 11

3. Fixed income risk management using duration and convexity

  • Duration: Lecture 7
  • Convexity: Lecture 8
  • Risk management and immunization: Lecture 9
